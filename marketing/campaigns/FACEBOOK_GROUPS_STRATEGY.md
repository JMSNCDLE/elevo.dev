# ELEVO — Facebook Groups Strategy

## Target Groups

- "Small Business UK" groups (dozens, 50K–300K members each)
- "Female Entrepreneurs UK/US/AU"
- "Restaurant Owners Network"
- "Hair Salon Business Tips"
- "Plumbers & Trades Business"
- "Dentists in Business"
- "Local Business Owners [City]" (search for your target cities)

---

## The Screenshot Method (highest conversion)

1. Generate content for a real business using ELEVO
2. Screenshot the output (GBP post for a plumber, ROAS report, etc.)
3. Post the screenshot with this copy:

> "I built a tool that does this in 60 seconds — [screenshot of real output for a real business type]
>
> Anyone who wants to try it on their business, link in comments."

**The screenshot does the selling. People see their own business in it.**

---

## Response Template (when people comment "how?")

> "It's called ELEVO — it's an AI tool built for local businesses. You enter your business details and it generates everything: Google posts, blog content, review replies, social captions. It also analyses your ad spend, finances, and Google ranking. 7-day free trial: [link]"

---

## Content Calendar for Groups (4 posts/week across all groups)

**Monday** — Value post: tip about Google rankings, ROAS, or review strategy. No direct CTA.

**Wednesday** — Screenshot post: real ELEVO output for a business in that niche. Soft CTA in comments.

**Friday** — Question post: "What's the biggest time sink in your business right now?" — engage replies, mention ELEVO organically when relevant.

**Sunday** — Story post: specific result ("A salon owner in Dublin cut their content time from 4 hours to 20 minutes"). Soft CTA at end.

---

## Rules

1. **Join groups at least 2 weeks before posting** — build context first by commenting on others' posts
2. **Never post the same content to multiple groups on the same day**
3. **Always reply to every comment on your posts within 2 hours**
4. **Never use affiliate-style language** — "I made £X" type posts get flagged
5. **Screenshot posts work best on Wednesday/Thursday** — highest group engagement
